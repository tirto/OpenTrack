Becky Pernicka

Systems

Becky Pernicka is a half-time UNIX systems administrator with direct experience with DECstations running Ultrix, HP Apollo 730s running HP-UX, and IBM RS6000s running AIX. She provides both hardware and software maintenance and upgrades as well as day-to-day user maintenance and support. She has experience with PC hardware and software support. She is currently responsible for UNIX workstations used by the Aerospace Engineering and Materials Engineering programs. The Aerospace computers are used by junior, senior, and graduate level students for graduate thesis research, senior aircraft and spacecraft design projects, classroom assignments, e-mail, and access to the World Wide Web. The computers run Matlab, Ansys, Mechanica, Ideas Master Series, AAA (Advanced Aircraft Analysis), STK (Satellite Toolkit), Liken (a Mac emulator), SoftPC (a PC emulator), Netscape, Fortran, and C compilers. Professors have also acquired several unsupported programs from NASA and from other universities. The Materials computers are used by graduate students for simulation software, e-mail, and access to the Web. The computers run T-Suprem4 and other simulation software and Netscape.
